# 🏗 Our Project Overview

“We are building a spatial welfare platform for cities, developers, planners, and climate forecasting.” - Gerver

The idea is to build “something” within the boundaries of pricing land under the assumption of the environmental risks plus the potential for development.

The idea is to have three phases.

---

## Phase I:

**The R&D** - This is where we prove that the idea is economically viable and technically, within our reach.

As team members we would get to learn and do research by combining environmental health risk and the potential for development in pre-selected locations. This also will include collecting data of pollution, housing prices, development indicators such as permits, zoning, transit projects) and ultimately, have a clear framework.

---

## Phase II:

**The beta model** - This phase is critical for the hands-on experience and healthy development of our project. This basically will turn the research we have done during Phase I, into a functional and operational model that can quantify the adjusted welfare adjusted to the value of land.

### Main objectives:

1. Development of an Environmental Risk Index (pollution, toxicity, and health related issues)  
2. Development of an Index for Potential Development (permits, planned infrastructure, and job opportunities)  
3. Start estimating the marginal Willingness to Pay (WTP) using Difference in Differences, boundaries, or IV. This should allow us to start mapping coefficients into dollar values per each unit of pollution, and/or the dollar per each unit of amenity  
4. Finally, create a simple version of a Minimum Value Product (MVP). That is, not perfect and just functional enough to test it.

The idea is one city, where we have a clean dataset, one regression model, and one simple map being produced.

---

## Phase III - The End Goal (End of Fall 2026):

**Presenting / Pitching out Idea** - We will be communicating our value proposition and how viable our project will be in the real world.

For this, we will be using the SCET network and assistance. The idea is to communicate the value proposition for developers, planners, and forecasters.
